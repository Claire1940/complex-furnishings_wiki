<Section icon={Target} title="Core Gameplay: Selling Furniture and Survival">

The primary objective in **complex furnishings** revolves around a high-risk, high-reward economy. Players must venture deep into the stockroom to find valuable pieces of furniture. However, the further you go, the more the environment resists your presence. This resistance manifests as shifting layouts, flickering lights, and the appearance of dangerous entities.

Selling furniture is not as simple as clicking an item. You must physically move or interact with objects and navigate them back to a designated "Safe Zone" or storefront area. This requires a deep understanding of the room layouts and the fastest routes back to safety.

| Activity | Description | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scavenging** | Searching for chairs, tables, and decor in the stockroom | **High** |
| **Navigation** | Marking paths to avoid getting lost in infinite loops | **Critical** |
| **Selling** | Returning items to the store front for currency | **High** |
| **Evasion** | Avoiding entities that spawn when items are moved | **Medium** |

<Callout type="info" title="Economic Strategy">
Focus on smaller, high-value items like lamps or side tables during your first few runs. They are easier to carry and allow for faster movement when an entity begins a chase sequence.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Entry">
    Enter the stockroom through the main double doors. Do not sprint immediately; listen for the hum of the lights and any distant footsteps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify Landmarks">
    Find a unique piece of furniture—like a red sofa or a specific painting—and use it as a "North Star" for your return trip.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Looting Protocol">
    Pick up an item. If the lights flicker or the ambient noise stops, drop the item and hide behind a shelf. This indicates the stockroom is reacting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Extraction">
    Move toward the exit using the landmarks you identified. If pursued, use tight corners to break the entity's pathfinding.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Danger Zone">
Avoid the "Dark Rooms" sections unless you have a light source. Entities in these areas are significantly more aggressive and can detect players from a greater distance.
</Callout>

The map of **complex furnishings** is not static. While certain "anchor rooms" remain consistent, the corridors connecting them can feel like they are shifting. Understanding the visual language of these rooms is vital for long-term survival.
