<Section icon={Target} title="Core Gameplay Mechanics and Selling Guide">

The primary objective in **complex furnishings new game** is to profit from the infinite inventory of the stockroom. Success requires a deep understanding of item values and the mechanics of transporting furniture back to the "Safe Zone" or selling points. Every item you encounter has a weight and a potential value, making inventory management a critical skill.

| Item Category | Common Locations | Value Rating | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Unique Artifacts** | Hidden Rooms, End Zones | Very High | High |
| **Industrial Racks** | Loading Docks | Moderate | Low |

To maximize your earnings, you must prioritize items that offer the best value-to-weight ratio. Large furniture pieces like sofas and wardrobes often require multiple players to move efficiently, highlighting the importance of the co-op tag. If you are playing solo, focusing on smaller, high-value electronics or decor items is often the safer and more profitable route.

<Callout type="tip" title="Efficiency Tip">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Tactics and Entity Management">

Survival in the **complex furnishings new game** isn't just about running away; it's about awareness and preparation. The infinite stockroom is home to various entities that respond to noise, movement, and the presence of players. Understanding how to navigate these threats is the difference between a successful haul and a lost run.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Scouting">
    When entering a new room, stay crouched and listen for audio cues. Entities often emit distinct humming or mechanical sounds before they become visible.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Landmark Marking">
    The stockroom is repetitive. Use large, distinct furniture clusters or specific wall patterns as landmarks to ensure you can find your way back to the exit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Resource Management">
    Keep an eye on your stamina. Sprinting should be reserved for escaping entities, not for general traversal. Over-exertion will leave you vulnerable during a chase.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Group Coordination">
    In co-op play, designate one player as the "Lookout." This player should stay unencumbered to watch for threats while others move the furniture.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Escape Phase">
    Once you have your items, move directly toward the nearest known safe zone. Do not linger to explore "one more room" if your inventory is full.
  </Step>
</Steps>
