Survival in the **complex furnishings backrooms** is less about combat and more about environmental awareness. The "entities" in this game are often subtle—shadows in the periphery or sudden changes in the room layout. To survive long-term, you must adopt a methodical approach to exploration.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Advanced Navigation: Mapping the Infinite">

Experienced players in the **complex furnishings backrooms** community have developed specific "Routes" to maximize their efficiency. While the map is procedurally generated or shifting, certain logic remains consistent. Using the "Right-Hand Rule" (keeping your hand on the right wall) is a classic survival tactic that works surprisingly well here.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Sector Tagging">
    Mentally divide the stockroom into sectors based on the type of furniture present (e.g., "The Sofa Sector").
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Sound Mapping">
    Different areas of the stockroom have different ambient noises. Use these to determine if you are getting closer to the "Exit" or deeper into the "Void."
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Team Scopes">
    Have one player stand in a doorway to prevent it from closing or shifting while the other explores the room. This "Door Wedge" tactic is vital for deep runs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Always look up. The ceiling tiles and lighting patterns in the stockroom often repeat in ways that the floor furniture does not. Mapping the ceiling can sometimes provide a clearer path than mapping the cluttered floor.
</Callout>
