Identifying **complex furnishings enemies** before they strike is the difference between a successful haul and a permanent stay in the stockroom. These entities do not follow standard Roblox horror tropes; they are designed to exploit the "uncanny valley" of a retail environment.

The entities often manifest as distortions of the furniture store theme. For instance, a shelf that was empty a moment ago might suddenly be stocked with items that seem "wrong" in texture or color. These are early warning signs that an entity is manifesting in your immediate vicinity.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Identification Tip">

<Section icon={Target} title="Survival Strategies and Evasion Tactics">

Surviving encounters with **complex furnishings enemies** requires a disciplined approach to movement and inventory management. Because the game is built for co-op play, your strategy must involve your entire team. Splitting up is often a death sentence, as the stockroom can dynamically alter its layout to isolate lone players.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish a Perimeter">
    Before entering a new section of the stockroom, designate a "scout" and a "navigator." The scout looks for furniture to sell, while the navigator keeps track of the exit route.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Monitor Aggression Levels">
    Pay attention to the environmental cues. If the humming of the fluorescent lights becomes deafening, it means the stockroom is reaching a high state of hostility.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Silent Extraction">
    When moving furniture, avoid dragging items across the floor. The noise alerts nearby entities. Lift items together in co-op to minimize sound.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Utilize Safe Zones">
    Certain areas with brighter, stable lighting act as temporary safe zones. Entities rarely enter these highly lit "checkpoints."
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Navigating the Hostile Environment">

The environment itself is one of the most dangerous **complex furnishings enemies**. The stockroom is infinite, meaning traditional mapping is impossible. Instead, you must rely on "Liminal Navigation"—using the repetitive nature of the rooms to your advantage.

Entities often use the maze-like structure to corner players. By understanding the common room layouts, you can predict where an entity might try to intercept you. Always look for "transitional spaces" like hallways or doorways that offer multiple exit points.

<Callout type="warning" title="Navigation Hazard">
Do not trust the "Exit" signs. In high-hostility phases, the stockroom will flip these signs to lead players deeper into the infinite void.
</Callout>
