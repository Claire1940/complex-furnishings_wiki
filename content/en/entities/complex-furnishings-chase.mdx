<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Tactics for the Complex Furnishings Chase">

Surviving a **complex furnishings chase** requires more than just fast reflexes; it demands a deep understanding of the stockroom's layout. Entities in this game do not follow simple patrol paths. Instead, they react to player noise and the amount of furniture being moved. When a chase begins, the audio cues—flickering fluorescent lights and distorted humming—are your only early warnings. You must transition from a "collecting" mindset to an "evasion" mindset instantly. Navigating around dense furniture clusters is the most common point of failure for new players, as getting snagged on a stray couch or shelf often leads to an immediate game over.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Exploration & Landmark Marking">
    Enter the stockroom slowly. Before picking up any items, identify at least two exit routes. Use distinct objects like a **bright red sofa** or a **triple-stacked shelf** as mental landmarks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Quiet Collection">
    Pick up furniture items one at a time. Dragging multiple items creates significantly more noise, which drastically increases the chance of triggering a chase sequence early in your run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Recognizing Audio Triggers">
    Listen for the "low-frequency hum" change. If the background noise suddenly cuts out or shifts in pitch, an entity has likely spawned in a nearby room. Drop your items and find a wide-open area.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Chase Execution">
    When the chase is active, do not run in a straight line if the room is cluttered. Weave through shelves to break the entity's line of sight. Entities in Complex Furnishings have high top speeds but struggle with tight, 90-degree turns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Regrouping in Co-op">
    If playing with a team, use one player as a "distraction" while others move the high-value furniture toward the extraction point. This division of labor is the most efficient way to maximize profit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Chase Phase | Warning Signals | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stalking** | Distant footsteps, light flickering | Stop moving, crouch, and listen |
| **Detection** | Sharp screech, music shift | Drop heavy items, sprint to open space |
| **Active Chase** | Visual distortion, entity proximity | Use 90-degree turns, break line of sight |
| **Cool Down** | Silence, lights stabilize | Return to previous landmark, wait 10 seconds |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Movement Tip">
Sprinting consumes stamina. In Complex Furnishings, stamina regenerates slowly. Only use your sprint in short bursts during the active **complex furnishings chase** to ensure you don't run out of breath in a dead-end corridor.
</Callout>
