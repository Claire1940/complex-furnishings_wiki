<Section icon={Swords} title="Threat Directory: Complex Furnishings Entities Guide">

Surviving the depths of the infinite stockroom requires a comprehensive understanding of the threats you will face. Each of the **complex furnishings entities** possesses unique behavioral patterns, auditory triggers, and patrol routes. Treating every threat with the same approach will quickly lead to a failed run.

The entities are designed to exploit player panic. When a chase begins, the maze-like structure of the stockroom makes it easy to get cornered. By studying the specific behaviors outlined below, your team can react calmly and execute effective counter-strategies.

| Entity | Danger Level | Behavior Pattern | Counter Strategy |
| Stalker | High | Follows player sounds and visual cues | Move slowly, crouch, and avoid running |
| Lurker | Medium | Hides behind furniture clusters | Check corners before entering new rooms |
| Screamer | High | Triggers sudden chase sequences | Sprint to open pathways and break line of sight |

### The Stalker
The Stalker is a persistent threat that wanders the corridors. It relies heavily on sound detection. Walking or running near this entity will immediately draw its attention. When active, it emits a low-frequency hum that vibrates the screen slightly, giving alert players time to crouch and hide.

### The Lurker
Unlike the active patrol of the Stalker, the Lurker prefers stationary ambush tactics. It blends into the cluttered furniture piles and shadows of larger showrooms. Walking directly into its line of sight triggers an immediate attack. Checking corners and using your flashlight sparingly are the best ways to avoid this threat.

### The Screamer
The Screamer is an aggressive chaser that activates when players attempt to extract high-value furniture. It emits a piercing shriek before sprinting toward the nearest player. Speed is your only ally here; you must drop any heavy cargo immediately to outrun it.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Tactical Furniture Interaction and Item Management">

Furniture is not just your source of income; it is also a physical barrier that can save your life. Knowing how to interact with the environment is crucial when evading the hostile forces inside the store.

Carrying heavy items like wardrobes or sofas slows your movement speed significantly. This makes you highly vulnerable to any sudden entity spawns. Your team must constantly balance the greed of securing high-value stock with the necessity of staying agile.
